written instructions

Download written instructions

You will need:

3m of fabric - non stretch and lightweight. We used Rayon (love) (you can get away with less fabric but always good to have some spare and you can make a matching top & scrunchies!! Also if you are up cycling old clothes you can do the frill in a different material)

  • Cotton to match
  • Fabric Cutting Scissors
  • Paper cutting scissors (no they are not the same thing?!?!?!)
  • Friction marker pen (these are a MUST HAVE - SO HANDY IT’S INSANE ITEM)
  • Sewing Pins
  • Measuring tape
  • Seam Ripper (in case of mistakes)
  • Sewing Machine
  • A way to print our downloaded pattern on A4 paper
  • Sticky tape to join the pattern up

Not needed but good to have

  • Rotary Cutter + Cutting Mat (my preferred method of cutting the fabric)
  • Weights (or anything heavy-ish)
  • Tiny Trimming scissors
  • Seam ruler (super handy)
  • Safety pins

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17
PRINTING AND CUTTING THE PATTERN

= Download and print out pattern. Make sure you print ‘to size’. To check this simply measure the 3cm line on page 2 of your download.

= Cut all the way around the outside rectangle edge on each page.

= Now it’s time to tape them up… for a guide to how each pattern piece looks, refer to the guide on page 2 of the download

= Using the numbers to guide you, sticky tape together the pattern pieces together using the tabs, which are marked by the stripy lines.
+ you will know once you are ready to start a new pattern piece as the number will come back to 1

= Cut out each pattern piece, ending up with:

  • BACK PANEL
  • SIDE PANNEL
  • MAIN FRILL
  • TOP FRILL

= Lastly cut out each of the labels on the last page and put to one side

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17
CUTTING THE MATERIAL PATTERN PEICES

= For this project non-stretch light weight fabrics… we used a rayon which is a great natural fibre and has a lovely drape!

= Handy tip is always make sure you wash your fabric before you use it as it can shrink if you don’t - which is always a major bummer.

= Start by ironing your fabric. Be sure that you test the iron temperature on a small section of your fabric first.

= Before we dive into the main part of the skirt we are going to cut the brand of the skirt. Use the selvedge edge of the fabric (the edge that has a finished edge) as a guide. First cut away the frayed edge the measure 10cm in and cut two strips;

  • 10cm x 220cm
  • 10cm x 135cm

= Now cut each of the pattern pieces, we use a cutting board, fabric weights and a rotary cutter as it’s more accurate and faster but you can also use scissors . When cutting the pattern note;

  • How many of each pattern you need - written on the pattern
  • to place each pattern piece on grain. On grain is lining up the ‘place on grain line’ to either the selvedge edge or 90degrees along the cut edge.
  • when cutting ‘place on fold’ pattern pieces, fold fabric in half and line the place on fold side of the pattern up with the folded edge of the fabric. Be sure not to cut along the folded edge.
  • when cutting two pieces of the same pattern you can either fold the material in half and cut both at the same time OR flip the pattern, so cut one with the pattern facing up, then one with the pattern facing down.
  • Be slow and very careful when cutting the pieces as even slight movement in the fabric can cause issues later on.

Then you should end up with all the pieces cut…

  • Start by cutting ONE BACK PANEL (**place on fold to cut)
  • then Two SIDE PANNELS
  • then four MAIN FRILL (**place on fold to cut)
  • finally two TOP FRILLS

And your two waist bands

Remember
= Once you cut each pattern piece, safety pin (or regular pin) a label to each one so if they get separated from the pattern, you know what pieces they are.

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17

SEWING THE FRILL

= Next we will assemble the FRILL. First join the four sections of the MAIN FRILL together along the short side edge using a French seam. **always pin from the top of the frill and pin down to the bottom, so if there was any movement while cutting the pattern causing uneven joins you can trim the hem edge before sewing final hem.

= Then using a French Seam, join the TOP FRILL pieces to either end of the MAIN FRILL section you just sewed together.

= You now have your FRILL! YAY! Now we will join the frill to the skirt.

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17

SEWING THE MAIN SECTION OF THE SKIRT TO THE FRILL

= Fold the main section of your skirt in half and mark the middle curved bottom of your skirt with your handy dandy markable pen. (always make sure you check this irons off your fabric first)

= Fold your frill in half and mark the top middle edge.

= With right sides facing out line up the two centre marked points, then pin the skirt and frill together, starting from the middle and working out to the top on both edges.

= Sew the frill and skirt together with a 5/8 inch French seam.

= If there is any excess at the top of the skirt from movement while cutting the pattern, trim evenly.

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17

HEMING THE SKIRT

= Starting at the top of the skirt carefully trim off any frayed edges or uneven seam joins around the seam edge of the frill. Keep the trimming as minimal as possible.

= Hem the edge of your frill, you can use a rolled seam foot or we just use the below technique.

Rolled seam (without a rolled seam foot)

= Press about 1/2 cm the whole way around the seam, using your seam ruler to check you’re keeping it even

= Then come back around and roll in another 1/2cm so you encase the frayed edge. Pinning if you need to

= Then sew a 1/4 inch all the way around the double pressed seam!

= Now we are almost done - just have to sew the band on.

VIDEO TIMESTAMP 3.17

SEWING THE WAIST BAND

= First we create a hole for the tie to go through.

= Get your two long pieces and place right sides together.

= Mark every 2cm with your fabric pen.

= Sew a seam as close to the bottom of the waistband as you feel comfortable.

= To finish off you can sew a rectangle around the holes for the tie to make sure it doesn’t move.

= Press your waist band, cut off any loose threads and you are ready to hit the streets in your new skirt! Boom!!!!
